Daeth - Karl Aannestad

by: Karl Aannestad

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.

1. Why I Made This Game

I like playing infection tag in real life, and this game seemed fun to make and play.

2. What I Learned

Some testers said that it was too difficult, so I turned down the enemies' speed. However, I couldn't make it too easy, so I did just a little bit.

3. Architecture of My Game

The enemies' update method takes in a player object, and they update their coordinates based on where the player is. The player moves by using mouse listeners, which set the player's x and y to the mouse every time it moves, clicks, drags, or stays still. The power up appears on a timer, and when it touches the player (using a collision check in the update method, which takes in a player object), it disappears and adds to the power up count of the player. When the player activates the power up, the enemies' speed is set to zero and their color changes. After a few seconds, they turn back to red and their speed returns.

4. Technical Implementation

I like the warning system on the enemies, and also how the enemies move. These both took a really long time to figure out, so I'm not proud of that part, but I am proud of how they worked out in the end. The enemies time two seconds from their spawning, where they are orange, not red. Their speed is zero and they won't kill the player if they collide. This uses a warning boolean which, when true, will change all those settings on the enemy object. Then, after two seconds since spawn, the warning boolean is set to false, which causes the enemies to turn red, begin moving, and kill the player if they touch it. For the enemies' movement, they move in diagonals unless they are on a straight line to the player, in which case they move straight towards it. Earlier, they would move straight until they reached a 45 degree diagonal to the player, when they would begin to move diagonal. I changed this because it seems to work better the new way. I had to change how the player's x and y were checked by each enemy, but it works fine now.

5. Most Challenging Part

Figuring out problems with how the circle that represented the player was rendered. It was inconsistent, since sometimes it seemed like the center of the circle was the x and y (like it should be), but sometimes it was the top left corner. I found a way to measure this and finally figure out the problem, although I basically had to rewrite every part of that code so it made more sense.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

I would make the UI at the top look cooler, and add different kinds of power ups. I would also check how gameplay would go with the alternate system of enemy movement.
